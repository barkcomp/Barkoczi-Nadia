Biographical Note

Nadia Barkoczi, PhD is Lecturer at Technical University of Cluj-Napoca / Department of Teacher Training / Romania. Her research agenda is based on the study of how advanced learning technologies can generate pedagogical innovations, addressing technology in the circular framework of research, teaching, learning and assessment, where researchers, teachers and students are changing roles to ensure lifelong learning by interlinking technology with education. She is especially interested in exploring the interplay between technological innovations and professional development, focusing on strategies to prepare students and teachers for technology integration in educational practices, with research interests in adoption of artificial intelligence technologies in education, teacher training, models of acceptance of technologies, emerging technologies in education, personalized and adaptive learning, technology-enhanced learning, digital competencies, STEM education. She is a member of editorial boards and scientific international committees and constantly involved in research collaborations in the areas of teaching and digital skills training for students and teachers in a learning environment with unlimited possibilities to enhance high-order skills such problem-solving, critical thinking and creativity.
